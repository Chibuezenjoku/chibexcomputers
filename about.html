About

Dr. Chikwendu C. Ejike

is a native of Dioka Nzerem in Okigwe Senatorial District of Imo state. He is a BDS holder (Dental Surgery/Surgeon) from the Prestigious University of Benin (2005/6), a Fellow of the National Postgraduate Medical college of Nigeria in Dental/Maxillofacial Surgery (FNPMCN/FMCDS/OMFS) and an Associate Fellow of West African College of Surgeons (AFWACS). He is a fellow/consultant Oral and Maxillo-Facial Surgeon of both West African graduate Medical College of Nigeria (FMCDS). He is a member of both local and international Affiliate bodies like National Association of Oral and Maxillo-Facial Surgeons (NAOMS), Nigeria Medical Association (NMA), Nigeria Dental Association (NDA), International Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons (IAOMS) etc. He is a certified cleft lip and palate surgeon by "Partners in Africa Cleft Team consisting of Cranio-Maxillofacial hospital in Seattle Saunders, USA, Ghana, Ethiopia and Nigeria. He is also a certified Cleft Lip and Palate Surgeon by SmileTrain and has successfully completed and operated over five hundred (500), i.e; recorded/unrecorded Cleft lip and palate patients. He is the Former Coordinator and SmileTrain Chief Surgeon in VADEN SPECIALIST CLINIC AND MATERNITY, Owerri,Imo State. He was the Head of Department of General Surgery and Examination Officer, Madonna University Teaching Hospital, Elele Campus, Rivers State, Nigeria (2016 to 2019). He is also the Head of Department of DENTAL & MAXILLOFACIAL SURGERY DEPARTMENT, Federal Medical Centre, Owerri,Imo,State, Nigeria. The Founder/ President/ Chief Cleft Surgeon of GALAXY EASTERN CLEFTS CARE FOUNDATION AND GALAXY BRITISH HOSPITAL COMPLEX, Owerri, Imo State, Nigeria. He is married and blessed with children.


His Areas of esearch interest are:

  1. Congenital Cranio-Facial anomalies e.g. orofacial cleft lip and palate surgeries, repairs, awareness and nutritional education to parents of children with cleft lip and palate.
  2. Maxillofacial tumour ablations and Reconstruction surgeries.
  3. Craniofacial fractures.
  4. Orofacial cleft and possible aetiologies in African Communities.
  5. Common causes of oncologis pathologies amongst Urban and Rural dwellers.
  6. Cranio-facial soft and hard tissue infection.
  7. Evidence-based use of free tissue transfers (micro-vascular) in management of oro-facial tissue defects.
